Spencer Allan is Chicago-based interdisciplinary writer and designer studying Journalism, Architectural Engineering Design, and Art, Theory, Practice at Northwestern University. His work focuses on the intersection of art and engineering with storytelling, whether that means reporting a county election in rural Wisconsin, laser cutting an architectural model, or establishing a clothing brand with worldwide clientele. His work has been featured in institutions such as the Museum of Contemporary Art Chicago, and his typewriter collection includes a restored 1966 Hermes 3000 and his great-grandfather’s 1927 Royal Portable. When’s he’s not writing biographies in third person, you can probably find him riding the train around the city.

...with additional experience in web development, photo/video editing, laser cutting, sewing, service and industrial design methods.
